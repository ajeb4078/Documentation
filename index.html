Background

Food Bank Australia (FBA) is a food relief charity organisation that provides 77 million meals a year to Australians in need. Approximately one in five Australians have experienced food insecurity at some point in the last 12 months. Food insecurity is prevalent across the country and refers to when an individual doesnâ€™t have enough food for themselves or their family and cannot afford to buy more food.

Food Bank Australia works with a dedicated network of farmers, manufacturers, retailers and transport services to provide fresh food to those in need. They are not only involved with combating hunger FBA are heavily in bettering the nation. This is done through various educational programs in schools as well as committing to resolve the 20 billion dollar food waste problem in Australia.

FBA relies heavily on volunteer labour to pick, pack and distribute food around the country. Despite their best efforts, many still unfortunately go hungry and FBA continues to strive to reach the objective of eliminating food insecurity in Australia.

Research

The current Food Bank Australia site is initially overwhelming. Visually the bright purple and bold interface is overbearing and can be straining on the eyes of users (refer to image of landing page, figure 1.2).

The use of the abraham-regular font (from the logo) in all caps inconsistently across various pages makes the design appear random to users. This element was designed to be playful and engaging but its random nature has a more unprofessional or childish presence (refer to image of secondary page, figure 1.3).

Additionally, the layout and structure of the website is highly confusing. The large amounts of unnecessary pages and links decreases overall usability (refer to condensed site map, figure 1.4). Many pages had content that was not required or already featured on another portion of the site. The repeated content made the going through the site confusing and complicated. Most pages were quite short with limited scrolling and used links to provide users with added information. This meant that a user would sometimes click through 5 different pages to reach certain parts of the site.



User Profiles

Upon investigating the current Food Bank site, it is clear that 3 main users exist.

  1. The Donor: An individual that wishes to provide monetary support to the cause.

  2. The Volunteer: An individual that wishes to donate time towards the cause and help distribute packages.

  3. The Recipient: An individual that wishes to apply for food support and receive donations.

Ideation

In order to begin the redesign, it was important to identify which pages were most important for the Food Bank Australia site. A large portion of the current FBA site is directed towards explaining the mission of the charity and how it achieves certain objectives. However, this is not the primary users that Food bank Australia needs to cater for. It would be more effective for foodbank Australia to target donors and volunteers and simplify the processes of donation of money, food and time. The objective of the redesign is to increase use base generate a sense of community by having the application aspects front and centre of the webpage.

The 3 main objectives of the redesign is to:

  • Improve landing page making it more engaging.
  • Condense the amount of pages explaining the charity mission into an indepth 'about us' page.
  • Make the forms for donors, volunteers and recipients more accessible and usable.

Mood Boards

To initiate the design process mood boards were created to gather and generate inspiration for possible designs. There were two main mood boards one with possible landing pages and another to explore possible form styles.

Landing Page

The landing page design inspiration showed simple sleek interfaces. The page is going to feature large and bold initial text in a sans-serif font. It will also show a key asset to the right of the screen. The colours incorporated will be the signature Food Bank Australia Purple as well as a white and black secondary colours.

Further exploration into landing pages showed a need to engage users beyond a sleek interface. The landing page should also be dynamic with certain assets being animated to increase visual appeal for users.

Form Styles

A mood board was also generated to explore different form styles. Since donors, volunteers and recipients all will be required to fill in forms on the new redesigned site. The main design is simple minimalistic blocks to enter details. A progress bar increases usability as it shows what part of the form users are up to.
